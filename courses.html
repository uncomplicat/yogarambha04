1. Teachers' Training

Yoga Teachers' Training 200 Hrs

Registered under Yoga Alliance (RYT 200), this training is the flagship program of the Yogarambha method. Evolved over a decade through meticulous observations and recalibration, this YTT includes a comprehensive teaching of all the relevant aspect of a technically sound, anatomically safe and meditatively progressive a yoga practice. This intensive course not only covers the generic aspects of being a yoga teacher such as practice, teaching methodology, anatomy and physiology but also safety and first-aid protocol for eventualities and modifying the Yogarambha method for prenatal-postnatal students.

Short Term Teachers' Training

Yogarambha believes in making the practice of yoga accessible to all. We specialise in short term teacher’s training in Vinyasa and Pranayama which can be registered under “continual learning” RYT 200 under Yoga Alliance. Our short courses are perfect for the teachers looking to upgrade their knowledge, learn safer movement patterns and teaching methodology and for the practitioner who would like to deepen their practice and understand what they are actually doing on their mat. Our short-term courses form the perfect bridge between serious practitioners who want to understand the nuances of the subject but don’t have the intention of doing a full-fledged teacher’s training yet. However, our past experience shows, most of our students from these courses have at some point of time taken to teaching both as hobby and as profession.

We currently host these YTT: • 75 Hrs Yoga Teacher’s Training, Vinyasa Yoga (The Yogarambha Method condensed (Vinyasa + Pranayama), Tri-Flow Module) • 50 Hrs Yoga Teacher’s Training, Vinyasa Yoga • 50 Hrs Pranayama Teacher’s Training (With Extensive focus on Tibetan yogic practice of Maha Moksha Sadhana) • 25 Hrs Yoga Teacher’s Training, Vinyasa Yoga

2. Student Training

Workshops

We offer a wide array of workshops across India and abroad on plethora of topics for both spectrum of students, those who want to begin their yoga practice right and those who want to practice advanced techniques safely. We have divided our workshops into three series. Each targeting a special group and a stage in physical and spiritual evolution.

Therupatic Bodyworks

Through an amalgamation of traditional and modern techniques of Toksen, chiropractic spinal releases, trigger points and myofascial release, the bodywork branch helps in releasing stubborn muscular and fascial knots and maintaining optimal mobility in the body. This practice is beneficial in chronic back, neck and knee pain and gait reset.

3. Yoga retreats

Advanced Pranayama Retreats

This retreat is specifically designed for advanced practitioners of pranayama who would like to be initiated into the ancient Tibetan pranayama and meditation techniques such as Tibetan fire breathing and Tummo meditation. This retreat is usually popular with yoga teachers and serious students who have already been practicing the breath conditioning and core stability exercises.

Yogabhyasa

A beginner – Intermediate Yoga immersion retreat where we decode the practice of the breath-oriented movement, primal movement and techniques of pranayama. An all-inclusive program, this retreat can be modified for all age groups and physical capacities.

Aranya - The School of Life

Drawing from the ancient gurukul ashram system, Aranya retreats are a rustic movement-oriented retreats which usually involve silent treks, camping and body application in wilderness which we have come to call ‘natural body conditioning’, all of this weaved into a balance through the thread of yoga and pranayama.
